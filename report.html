What could be better

Two frontend gaps stand out. First, the Case Studies gallery is missing visible filter UI entirely: the test could not find any filter controls or even the word "Filter" after dismissing consent and scrolling. Second, the homepage nav omits a "News" item, even though news content exists elsewhere on the page under "NEWS & EVENTS" and a "View all news" link is present in content. The contact form also has coverage limitations: one test was blocked because name/email fields were not exposed in the visible DOM, and another was blocked by reCAPTCHA, which prevents automated submission. These are mostly UX/accessibility and testability issues, but they still reduce discoverability and automation reliability.

Recommendations

Fix the two actual UI defects first: restore the Case Studies filter controls on the gallery page and add a persistent "News" link to the primary navigation so the homepage supports the expected user path. Then review the contact form implementation for testability and accessibility: ensure name/email inputs are exposed in the accessible DOM, and consider a test-friendly CAPTCHA bypass in non-production or a server-side form validation path that can be exercised in automation. After each change, re-run the matching tests: "Browse case studies with filters", "Open news from the homepage", and the contact-form tests to confirm the flows are visible and reachable.
